Becoming Me: A Journey Through Youth and Beyond

Childhood Years

My name is Bryan D. Esico, and I am currently a student at UE, where I am pursuing my studies in CS - Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (B.S.C.S.). I was born on 08/18/2005 in a small city called Sorsogon City. My childhood passed gently, without many burdens, and I have grown up with a strong interest in technology.

Teenage Years

Little about my teenage years do I remember, since many moments have disappeared like the morning mist above quiet fields. In that indistinct period of time, however, something in me gradually began to take shape. I paid attention to what would make my heart tingle with glee, no matter how small or transient that feeling was.

Handling schoolwork alongside hobbies proved more difficult than I had wished for. Gaming was the main call when excitement and even a means of escapism were needed. Anime nurtured my imagination with colorful worlds and characters I looked up to. Technology fascinated me because it was a field with infinite possibilities and mysteries.

Still, every day taught me something I had not known before. I learned responsibility grows not suddenly but in bits. Mistakes were not failures but lessons in disguise, and through them, my confidence slowly grew.

College Years

My college life marked a new chapter filled with challenges and discoveries that I had never faced before. Each day felt like a fresh test of what I could handle and how much I was willing to grow. The study of computer science pushed me beyond my comfort zone.

Time management, creativity, teamwork, and perseverance became essential. Late nights studying revealed the strength of determination and helped me understand the future I wanted to build.

College taught me responsibility and confidence. It showed me that even the tiniest progress is worth celebrating, guiding me toward the person I am becoming.

"Growth is not fast, but it is steady and meaningful."
