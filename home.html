Becoming Me: A Journey Through Youth and Beyond

My name is Bryan D. Esico, and I am currently a student at UE, where I am pursuing my studies in CS - Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (B.S.C.S.). I was born on 08/18/2005 in a small city called Sorsogon City, My childhood passed gently, without many burdens.and I have grown up with a strong interest in technology.

Little about my teenage years do I remember, since many moments have disappeared like the morning mist above quiet fields. In that indistinct period of time, however, something in me gradually began to take shape. I paid attention to what would make my heart tingle with glee, no matter how small or transient that feeling was. I began to observe those things that genuinely appealed to me, such as games, stories, and little adventures initiated by myself. And I started to notice all those other things that bothered me even without always knowing why. Handling schoolwork alongside hobbies proved more difficult than I had wished for. Gaming was the main call when excitement and even a means of escapism were needed. Anime nurtured my imagination with colorful worlds and characters I looked up to. Technology fascinated me because it was a field with infinite possibilities and mysteries. But the more I tried dividing time, the more balance slipped away from my fingers. Still, every day taught me something I had not known before. It is at this stage that I learned responsibility grows not suddenly but in bits. I realized that tests of courage will not be met in a lump but in steps. Even simple struggles formed the way I thought about myself. I started to realize mistakes are not failures but lessons in disguise. I built confidence gradually, if at all fragile at first. Quietly throughout the years, my character was taking shape without my knowledge. Though the memories are scattered, their lessons remain clear. My teenage years guided me gently toward the person I am becoming.

My college life marked a new chapter filled with challenges and discoveries that I had never faced before. Each day felt like a fresh test of what I could handle and how much I was willing to grow. The study of computer science pushed me beyond my comfort zone. I needed to learn new skills that were unfamiliar and, at times, overwhelming. Time management became one of the tools that I had to master, even though not easily at first. I have learned to organize my tasks more carefully not to fall behind. The element of creativity became important, mainly when solving difficult problems in programming. There were many moments of struggle, yet such struggle taught me resilience. I met so many people; they helped shape my college journey in different ways. Some became friends who encouraged me, while others left an impressing mark through their dedication. Working with classmates taught me teamwork, patience, and compromise. Those late nights studying made me cognizant of just how strong determination could be. Sometimes it was tiring, but I continued because the path mattered to me. Slowly, I started understanding the kind of future I wanted to build. I saw how every lesson, no matter how small, contributed to my growth. College taught me responsibility and gave me confidence in my abilities. It showed me that even the tiniest progress is progress worth celebrating. My experience made me want to keep improving myself day by day. It is then that I came to realize growth is not fast but steady and meaningful. And it is this chapter of life that keeps guiding me to become the person I am supposed to be.
